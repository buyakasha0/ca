What is Poverty?

Poverty is about not having enough money to meet basic needs including food, clothing, and shelter. However, poverty is much more than just not having enough money. The World Bank Organization describes poverty in this way: â€œPoverty is the lack of being able to eat. Poverty is lack of shelter. Poverty is the condition in which individuals or groups lack the financial resources and access to basic necessities needed for a decent standard of living. These necessities often include food, clean water, shelter, healthcare, education, and clothing. Poverty can manifest in various forms, ranging from absolute poverty (where people lack the basic necessities to survive) to relative poverty (where people may have enough for survival but still struggle compared to others in their society).

Global Poverty Statistics

A lot of people live in poverty around the world. In Ireland alone, around 560,000 people live in poverty, and globally, over 700 million people live in poverty. This is 10% of the world population, and they survive on less than 1 euro a day. According to the United Nations, efforts are being made to help these people by increasing access to healthcare, education, clean water, and economic opportunities.

Raising Awareness About Poverty

The Reality of Poverty

Poverty affects millions of people around the world. It manifests in different forms, including lack of access to basic necessities such as food, clean water, and shelter.

Children in Poverty

Children living in poverty are particularly vulnerable. They often lack proper nutrition, healthcare, and education, which can affect their entire future.

Rural Poverty

Rural poverty is another issue. People in remote rural areas often have limited access to resources like clean water, education, and healthcare.

Impact of Global Crises

Global crises, such as pandemics or conflicts, can push vulnerable communities deeper into poverty, making it harder to escape poverty during these times.
